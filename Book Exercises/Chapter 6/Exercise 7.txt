Title: People

Exercise: Start with the program you wrote for Exercise 6-1 (page 102).
Make two new dictionaries representing different people, and store all three
dictionaries in a list called people Loop through your list of people As you
loop through the list, print everything you know about each person.

Answer:

people = []

dict_person = {'first_name': 'John', 'last_name': 'Doe', 'age': 35, 'city': 'London'}
people.append(dict_person)

dict_person = {'first_name': 'Luke', 'last_name': 'Shaw', 'age': 31, 'city': 'Glasgow'}
people.append(dict_person)

dict_person = {'first_name': 'Christ', 'last_name': 'Lookman', 'age': 28, 'city': 'Boston'}
people.append(dict_person)

for person in people:
  name = f"{person['first_name'])} {person['last_name']}"
  age = person['age']
  city = person['city'].title()

  print(f"{name}, from {city}, is {age} years old.")
